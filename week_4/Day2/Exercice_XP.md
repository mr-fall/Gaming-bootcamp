# Video Game Studies: Core Concepts Assignment

## Section 1: History and Chronology

### Question 1: The Crash and The Revival
- **1983 Video Game Crash:**  
  Market oversaturation, poor-quality games, declining consumer confidence, and overproduction. Notorious example: *E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial*.
- **Reviving Company & Console:** Nintendo – NES (Nintendo Entertainment System)
- **Famous Mascot:** Mario

### Question 2: Era Differentiation
- **Golden Age of Arcades (1970–1983):**  
  - Key Game: *Pac-Man*  
  - Social Setting: Public (arcades)
- **Modern 3D & Online Era (2000–2010):**  
  - Key Game: *World of Warcraft*  
  - Social Setting: Private/Online (home)
- **Fundamental Difference:** Public social gameplay vs private/online social interaction

### Question 3: The 16-bit Wars
- **Main Companies & Consoles:**  
  - Sega – Genesis/Mega Drive  
  - Nintendo – SNES
- **Pivotal PC Games:**  
  - *Doom* (First-Person Shooter)  
  - *Civilization* (Turn-Based Strategy)

## Section 2: Genre Classification & Evolution

### Question 4: Genre Definition and Examples
- **Metroidvania:** Exploration-focused 2D/2.5D platforming with interconnected maps and ability-based progression  
  *Example:* Castlevania: Symphony of the Night
- **JRPG (Japanese Role-Playing Game):** Story-driven RPG with turn-based combat and narrative emphasis  
  *Example:* Final Fantasy VI
- **Real-Time Strategy (RTS):** Real-time resource management, unit building, and combat  
  *Example:* StarCraft

### Question 5: Understanding Hybridization
- Roguelike (e.g., Nethack) features procedural generation and permadeath.  
- Roguelite (e.g., Hades) maintains procedural levels but allows persistent progression between deaths, reducing penalty.  
- **Key Difference:** Meta-progression.
